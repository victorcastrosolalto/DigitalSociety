Executive Overview

The emergence of the digital society, often referred to as the Fourth Industrial Revolution, represents a fundamental shift in human interaction. This transformation is built upon the foundation of binary code, where all data is represented as a sequence of 1s and 0s.

A critical challenge is the "digital divide"—gaps in access between privileged groups and those disadvantaged by economic status, age, or geography. Understanding this society requires distinguishing between digitization, digitalization, and digital transformation.

Understanding the Digital Society

The Logic of 1s and 0s

Data Formats

Text: TXT, CSV
Audio: MP3, WAV
Video: MP4, AVI
Image: JPG, PNG

Steganography

Hiding secret text within binary code of images. Used for copyright (watermarks) or illicit communications.

Analogue vs. Digital

Analogue
  • • Continuous physical signals
  • • Waves / Needle displays
  • • Infinite continuum of values
Digital
  • • Discrete signals (1s and 0s)
  • • Finite set of values
  • • Reliable and less expensive

The Conversion Cycle

ADC
Analogue to Digital

Speaking into a microphone converts sound waves into data.

DAC
Digital to Analogue

Speakers convert digital files back into audible sound waves.

Processes of Digital Change

Digital Preservation

A comprehensive strategy ensuring long-term access to digital materials despite rapid technological obsolescence. This involves reformatting, secure archiving, and ensuring future compatibility.
