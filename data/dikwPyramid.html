The Core Insight

The DIKW Pyramid provides a roadmap for creating value. While you can have data without information, you cannot have information without data. Each level builds upon the last.

"You can have data without information, but you cannot have information without data." — Daniel Keys Moran

The Four Levels Explained

1. Data

Raw & Unorganized Facts

The foundation consists of raw facts, figures, numbers, or characters that lack context. Alone, they have no meaning.

Student Example

75

Is it temperature? A price? An age? We don't know.

2. Information

Context & Structure

Created by processing and organizing data. It answers the questions: Who, What, Where, When?

Student Example

A test score of 75%

The number now has context and becomes workable.

3. Knowledge

Meaning & Application

Deriving meaning and applying it to achieve goals. It focuses on the question: How?

Teacher Example

Assessing student success

Using results to determine if the student met the goal.

4. Wisdom

Insight & Predictions

Applying knowledge with insight to make predictions and informed actions. It answers: Why?

Teacher Example

Predicting a final grade

Intervening or adjusting instruction based on patterns.
